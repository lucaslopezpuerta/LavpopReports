Using the Lavpop Dashboard

The dashboard offers two main pages:

  • index.html – interactive charts and key performance indicators. Scroll through the sections to view each chart. Use the View Data Tables button in the header to switch to the table view or the Help button to return here.
  • data-tables.html – sortable and searchable tables with export options. Use the tabs across the top of the page to switch datasets, the search box to filter rows, and the CSV/PDF buttons to download data. Click Back to Dashboard in the header to return to the charts.

Chart Guide

Each chart in the dashboard visualizes a different metric. Hover over the charts to see exact values. Key visualizations include:

  • Monthly Revenue & Utilization Trend – bars show monthly net revenue while the line shows machine utilization percentage. Look for seasonal changes. Utilization is calculated as (cycles completed / available cycles) × 100.
  • Weekly Performance Comparison – compares the current week with the previous week and the 4‑week average. Net revenue and utilization are plotted together. Percentage change is ((current - previous) / previous) × 100.
  • Current Week vs 4‑Week Average Growth – highlights how much this week differs from the recent average. Positive bars mean growth above the average.
  • Revenue Distribution by Day of Week – daily revenue bars overlaid with a line for the 4‑week average. Identify your strongest and weakest days.
  • Best vs Worst Day Comparison – side‑by‑side metrics for the highest and lowest revenue days. Helps pinpoint what drives good performance.
  • Peak vs Off‑Peak Performance – contrasts busy hours with quieter periods. The difference percentage is ((peak - off‑peak) / off‑peak) × 100.
  • Hourly Revenue Distribution & 4‑Week Hourly Utilization – show when customers visit most often. The utilization charts use the formula above.
  • Capacity vs Revenue Efficiency – a scatter plot of utilization versus revenue efficiency (net revenue divided by utilization percentage). Points in the upper right signal the most profitable use of machines.
  • Customer Segment and Retention Charts – visualize how customer groups contribute to revenue and how many customers remain active. Average value per customer equals total revenue ÷ number of customers.
  • Marketing ROI Dashboard – tracks campaign performance. A simple ROI formula is (revenue from campaign - cost) ÷ cost. Values above zero indicate profit.
  • Payment Method Distribution – pie chart showing how customers pay. Percentages are (method total / overall revenue) × 100.
  • Weather Impact and Forecast – correlation charts show how weather metrics relate to revenue. The correlation coefficient ranges from -1 to +1. Values near ±1 imply a strong relationship. Forecast charts project future revenue using recent trends.
  • Revenue Concentration & Predictive Capacity – reveal which customer segments generate most revenue and suggest optimal machine capacity by hour.

Metric Definitions

The following formulas appear throughout the dashboard:

  • Net Revenue = total revenue − refunds and adjustments.
  • Utilization (%) = (completed cycles ÷ maximum possible cycles) × 100. Example: if 120 cycles run out of 240 available, utilization = 50%.
  • Revenue Efficiency = net revenue ÷ utilization %. Example: with R$500 net revenue and 20% utilization, efficiency = R$25 per utilization point.
  • Growth Rate = ((current − reference) ÷ reference) × 100. Used for week‑over‑week and month‑over‑month comparisons.
  • Average Value per Customer = total revenue ÷ number of unique customers.
  • Correlation Coefficient – measures how two variables move together. A value of 1 means perfect positive correlation, −1 perfect negative, and 0 no correlation.
